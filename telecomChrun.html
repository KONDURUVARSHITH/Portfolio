Forecasting Telecom Subscriber Turnover

Code Repository Report

Introduction

Customer churn represents a significant challenge in the telecommunications sector, directly impacting revenue and market position. Our research focuses on developing predictive models using machine learning techniques to identify potential customer churn and implement effective retention strategies.

Key Motivations:

  • Customer acquisition costs are 25 times higher than retention costs
  • Loyal customers generate stable revenue growth
  • Even small improvements in retention significantly impact profitability
  • Data-driven decision making can transform retention strategies

Dataset Analysis

Dataset Composition

  • 7,043 customer records
  • 21 distinct features
  • Comprehensive service information
  • Demographic data
  • Account history

Key Variables

  • Service subscriptions (phone, internet, security)
  • Contract details and payment methods
  • Customer demographics and history
  • Usage patterns and preferences

Exploratory Data Analysis

Demographic Insights:

  • Gender distribution is approximately equal
  • 16% are senior citizens
  • 50% have partners, 30% have dependents
  • Significant correlation between partner status and dependents

Customer Behavior Patterns:

  • Contract length strongly correlates with tenure
  • Monthly contracts show highest churn rates
  • Senior citizens demonstrate higher churn probability
  • Service charges significantly impact churn decisions

Methodology Implementation

Logistic Regression

Implemented binary classification with feature scaling, achieving 80.7% accuracy. Key findings:

  • Contract length is the strongest predictor
  • Service type significantly influences churn
  • Payment method impacts customer retention

Random Forest

Developed ensemble model with 80% accuracy. Notable insights:

  • Monthly contract type is the primary predictor
  • Tenure shows strong correlation with churn
  • Total charges provide significant predictive power

Support Vector Machine

Achieved highest accuracy at 82%. Key advantages:

  • Better handling of non-linear relationships
  • Improved performance on imbalanced data
  • More robust feature importance identification

Results and Analysis

Key Findings:

  • Contract length is inversely related to churn probability
  • DSL internet service shows lower churn rates than fiber optic
  • Total charges show complex relationship with churn
  • Service quality metrics strongly influence retention

Conclusions and Recommendations

Strategic Recommendations:

  • Focus on long-term contract incentives
  • Improve fiber optic service satisfaction
  • Develop targeted retention programs for high-risk segments
  • Implement proactive customer engagement strategies
